Treatment guide

What treatment helps ADHD?

There is no one perfect answer for everyone. Many people do best with a mix of support, such as medication, therapy, coaching, and simple day-to-day systems.

Download Pillr More guides

Main kinds of support

ADHD treatment often includes more than one thing. A clinician can help you choose what fits your needs, goals, and health situation.

  • Medication, when prescribed and monitored
  • Therapy or coaching for routines, planning, and habits
  • Sleep, stress, and environment changes
  • Simple external systems like reminders and trackers

Why systems matter

Even when medication helps, daily life still needs support. Reminders, logs, refill tracking, and one place to keep records can reduce avoidable friction.

What “working” usually looks like

Better treatment often means life feels more manageable. You may miss fewer tasks, stay more consistent, feel less overwhelmed, or have clearer check-ins with your clinician.

Keep it realistic

The goal is not to become perfect. The goal is to make daily life more steady, easier, and more repeatable.

What to track

  • When you took medication
  • Whether you missed, delayed, or skipped doses
  • How your day felt after
  • Any side effects or changes

Tracking this makes treatment decisions clearer over time.

Important note
