FAQ

Answers, without the overwhelm.

Does Pillr replace medical advice?

No. Pillr helps you track and organize medications, but it does not replace a clinician.

Can I track non-ADHD medications too?

Yes. Pillr supports any medications, with special timing tools for ADHD stimulants.

Where is my data stored?

Your data stays on device by default, with optional iCloud sync across Apple devices.

What can I export?

You can export medication history as CSV or PDF for easy sharing.

Is Pillr a medication management tool?

Pillr is built for medication management—set reminders, log doses, and review adherence right on your device so your ADHD routine stays steady.

Does Pillr include an ADHD symptom tracker?

Pillr keeps symptom context in mind by capturing focus windows, side-effect notes, and wear-off check-ins alongside medication data.

Can I use Pillr as a pill tracker or pill reminder?

Yes, Pillr combines pill tracker features with flexible reminders so you always know when to take, skip, or snooze a dose.

Why is Pillr called an ADHD app?

Every part of Pillr—from stimulant timing to focus timelines—was designed to support the specific rhythms of ADHD medication routines.

How does Pillr support medication adherence?

Pillr tracks adherence rates, streaks, and refill status, making it easy to share proof of consistency with clinicians or caregivers.

Can Pillr track stimulant schedules like Adderall, Vyvanse, or methylphenidate?

Pillr supports custom medication names and timing details, so you can track stimulant schedules such as Adderall, Vyvanse, or methylphenidate as directed by your clinician.

Is Pillr an iPhone app for ADHD medication reminders and pill tracking?

Yes. Pillr is an iPhone app designed for ADHD medication reminders, dose tracking, focus window logging, and adherence history exports.
