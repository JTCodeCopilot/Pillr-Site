Bearable vs Guava
for medication and symptom tracking

Bearable and Guava both connect medication records with symptoms, mood, pain, habits, and health trends. Bearable is centred on a flexible daily health journal, while Guava adds pill-supply tools, connected medical records, and broader visit preparation.

Published and last reviewed July 23, 2026

A note from Pillr

This article is published by Pillr. We make a medication tracking app, but this page compares Bearable and Guava for people deciding between those two options.

Quick answer

Choose Bearable if you want a highly customisable daily journal for medication, symptoms, side effects, mood, pain, habits, and personal health patterns.

Choose Guava if you want medication and symptom tracking beside pill counts, refill alerts, health records, patient-portal connections, visit preparation, and wider device integrations.

Both are available on iPhone and Android. Both have free access and optional paid features. The better fit depends on whether your priority is a focused daily journal or a broader personal health record.

Who this comparison is for

This comparison is for someone who wants to record medication beside symptoms, side effects, mood, pain, sleep, or daily habits. It is especially useful if the choice depends on pill-supply tracking, health-record connections, custom journal entries, reports, or appointment preparation.

It is not a guide to choosing medication, changing a dose, deciding whether a medicine caused a symptom, or interpreting a health correlation as medical proof.

Key differences

Bearable is daily-journal led. Its current medication guide describes medication reminders, dose entries, symptom groups, side-effect groups, reports, and comparison graphs inside a custom health journal.

Guava is personal-health-record led. Its current medication tracking guide describes schedules, reminders, pill counts, medication lists, symptom connections, and appointment records alongside patient portals and device data.

The overlap is substantial. Both can track medication, symptoms, mood, pain, habits, and trends. The practical difference is how much wider health information you want around the daily record.

Detailed comparison by use case

If medication reminders are the main starting point

Bearable lets you create multiple reminders for individual medications on different days, then log the time, dose, and number of doses taken. This works well if medication is one part of a custom daily health journal.

Guava combines medication schedules and reminders with completed entries, pill supply, and refill alerts. It may fit better if you want the reminder and the remaining supply to stay together.

If symptoms or side effects are the main record

Bearable gives symptoms, mood, pain, energy, habits, and other custom factors a central place. Its help guide suggests creating a separate symptom group for side effects and comparing those entries with medication records.

Guava also tracks symptoms, mood, pain, and medication effects. Its body map, health charts, and wider records may be more useful if location, medical history, or device data needs to sit beside the symptom log.

Patterns in an app can help organise questions, but they do not prove that a medication caused or improved a symptom.

If you want records for a healthcare appointment

Bearable offers reports, timelines, and export for sharing a clearer tracking history with a doctor or therapist.

Guava adds Visit Prep, questions, medical-record summaries, lab results, doctor notes, and patient-portal connections. This is the broader option when the appointment record needs more than daily tracking data.

If connected health data matters

Bearable lists Apple Health and Fitbit connections alongside automatic health-data syncing. Guava lists a wider set of health apps, fitness devices, glucose monitors, and patient portals.

Connected data does not guarantee every metric, record, provider, or device will sync. Check the current supported-source list before moving an established health record.

If privacy and account setup matter

Bearable says health data is encrypted on its servers and that it does not sell personal data. Its privacy policy explains that an account uses an email address and enables backup and restoring data across devices.

Guava uses an account to manage health information and connected records. Its privacy and security page says data is encrypted, users control sharing, and Guava does not sell health information.

Both apps hold sensitive health information. Review current account deletion, export, backup, analytics, and sharing rules before entering a full medication or medical history.

Where Bearable fits better

  • You want a custom daily journal for symptoms, mood, pain, habits, medication, and treatments.
  • You want to create your own symptom or side-effect groups.
  • You want medication to be one factor inside a wider daily wellbeing record.
  • You prefer reports and timelines without connecting a full medical-record system.

Where Guava fits better

  • You want medication reminders, pill counts, and refill alerts in the same app.
  • You want to connect patient portals, lab results, doctor notes, or medical documents.
  • You need wider device and health-app connections.
  • You want visit preparation built around both tracking entries and medical records.

When neither app may be enough

Bearable and Guava are broad health trackers. If your main need is an ADHD-specific medication routine with quick taken or skipped records, estimated focus windows, reflections, refill planning, and medication notes, Pillr is a separate option to consider.

What to verify before choosing

  • Whether the app supports every phone, tablet, browser, or connected device you plan to use.
  • Which history, insight, report, family, record, and AI features need a paid plan.
  • Whether medication reminders support your prescribed schedule and as-needed use.
  • Whether pill counts and refill alerts are needed in the same app.
  • Which patient portals, health apps, and device sources are supported in your region.
  • How account deletion, export, backup, analytics, and health-data sharing work.

Is Bearable or Guava better for medication and symptom tracking?

Bearable may fit better if you want a flexible daily journal for medication, symptoms, mood, pain, habits, and personal correlations. Guava may fit better if you also need pill counts, refill alerts, medical records, patient-portal connections, and visit preparation.

Do Bearable and Guava include medication reminders?

Yes. Both apps currently describe medication reminders and medication logs. Their wider workflows differ, so check whether you also need pill-supply tools, symptom reports, health-record connections, or custom daily tracking.

Can Bearable and Guava track side effects?

Both can record symptoms alongside medication. Bearable explains how to create a custom side-effect symptom group, while Guava connects medication entries with symptom, mood, pain, and health records.

Do Bearable or Guava replace medical advice?

No. They are tracking and record-keeping tools. Questions about doses, timing, side effects, interactions, symptoms, and missed medication need advice from a qualified healthcare professional.
