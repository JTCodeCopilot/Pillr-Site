Why telehealth prep matters

Telehealth is now part of adult ADHD care. A 2024 CDC report estimated that almost half of U.S. adults with current ADHD had used telehealth for ADHD services, and about one third had used it to obtain an ADHD medication prescription since March 2020.

That does not mean every appointment is easy. Video visits can be short, distracted, or squeezed between work and family. A written snapshot helps you avoid relying on memory alone.

Build a one-page dose snapshot

Before the appointment, gather the basics:

  • Current medication name and dose
  • Usual dose time and reminder setup
  • Taken, skipped, late, or missed doses since the last visit
  • Refill or supply problems
  • Any side effects or new concerns you want to discuss

The goal is not to create a perfect medical record. It is to give your clinician a clearer starting point.

Include the context around the dose

The National Institute of Mental Health describes adult ADHD symptoms as affecting work, home life, and relationships. For an appointment, context can be as useful as the dose log itself.

Add a few plain notes about what was happening on difficult days:

  • "Back-to-back meetings and forgot lunch"
  • "Reminder fired while driving, logged later"
  • "Slept badly before two missed morning doses"
  • "Could not fill prescription on time"

These notes can help separate a medication question from a routine, sleep, meal, refill, or workday problem.

Prepare questions before the call starts

Write your questions in advance, because they are easy to lose once the appointment begins. Useful questions might cover missed doses, side effects, refill timing, sleep, appetite, focus changes, or what to track before the next review.

NICE guidance says ADHD medication reviews can include how treatment works throughout the day, adverse effects, education or employment impact, missed doses, and physical or mental health changes. A short question list can keep those topics visible.

After the appointment, update the routine

When the visit ends, capture the next step while it is fresh:

  • Any follow-up appointment or refill date
  • What your clinician asked you to monitor
  • Any reminder, note, or tracking change you need to make
  • Questions to save for next time

Do not rely on "I will remember later." Make the next action visible before the day moves on.

What should adults with ADHD prepare before a telehealth medication appointment?

Prepare your current medication list, recent dose history, missed or delayed doses, side effects, sleep notes, refill issues, and the questions you want answered.

Can a dose log help during a telehealth ADHD visit?

A dose log can make the conversation more specific by showing what happened between visits. It does not replace clinical judgement or medical advice.

Should I change my ADHD medication before a video appointment?

Do not change medication timing, dose, or treatment based on a checklist. Ask your clinician or pharmacist what is appropriate for your prescription.

Can Pillr prepare clinical advice for my appointment?

No. Pillr helps with reminders, dose logging, notes, and medication history. It does not diagnose, treat, prescribe, or replace a clinician.
