Medisafe vs MyTherapy
for medication reminders

Medisafe and MyTherapy both combine medication reminders with dose records, refill support, family features, and reports. The practical difference is what you want around that routine: Medisafe puts more emphasis on care-partner alerts and medication-management tools, while MyTherapy puts more emphasis on a symptom, mood, pain, and measurement journal.

Published July 15, 2026 · Last reviewed July 17, 2026

A note from Pillr

This article is published by Pillr. We make a medication tracking app, but this page compares Medisafe and MyTherapy for people deciding between those two options.

Quick answer

Choose Medisafe if you want a medication-first app with reminders, as-needed and complex schedule support, refill alerts, care-partner notifications, reports, and a listed drug-interaction checker.

Choose MyTherapy if you want medication reminders beside a broader daily health journal for symptoms, pain, mood, measurements, injection sites, and appointment preparation.

Both are available on iPhone and Android, both can be downloaded free, and both list paid options in their app-store pages. Check the current store terms in your region before deciding.

Who this comparison is for

This comparison is for someone who wants more than a normal alarm: a medication schedule, a record of what happened, refill support, and something useful to review later.

It is especially useful if you are deciding whether care-partner alerts or broader symptom and measurement tracking matter more. It is not a guide to choosing medication, changing a dose, or deciding what to do after a missed dose.

Key differences

Medisafe is more medication-management led. Its current Google Play page lists complex schedules, as-needed medication, refill alerts, care-partner support through Medfriend, doctor reports, health measurements, and a drug-to-drug interaction checker.

MyTherapy is more health-journal led. Its official app page combines medication reminders and intake records with symptoms, mood, measurements, refill planning, appointment reminders, and reports.

There is overlap. Both now describe family support, refill tools, health measurements, and reports, so the decision is less about a simple feature count and more about which daily flow feels closer to the job you need done.

Detailed comparison by use case

If reminders and dose records are the main job

Both apps cover the basics. Medisafe describes medication alarms, flexible and complex schedules, as-needed medication, refill alerts, and a shareable logbook. MyTherapy describes medication alarms, an intake record, refill and prescription reminders, and a personal medication plan.

Before choosing, check whether the reminder can be postponed, whether a late entry keeps its actual time, and whether the history clearly distinguishes taken, skipped, and unrecorded doses. Those details can matter more than the number of features on a store page.

If a family member or caregiver needs visibility

Medisafe's Medfriend feature is the clearer fit when you specifically want a chosen person to receive an alert after a dose is not recorded. Its family tools can also support multiple profiles, although current plan limits should be checked in the app.

MyTherapy now describes family profiles for shared care. That may suit someone who wants several routines in one account, but confirm the current profile, sharing, and paid-feature rules before setting up a household.

If symptoms and measurements matter beside medication

MyTherapy puts symptoms, pain, mood, blood pressure, weight, blood sugar, and other measurements at the centre of its health journal. It also describes reminders for measurements and daily activities.

Medisafe also lists health measurements, but its public product information gives more attention to medication schedules, Medfriend, interaction information, refills, and medication reporting.

If you want something useful for an appointment

Medisafe describes daily, weekly, and monthly medication reporting with a logbook that can be shared with a doctor. MyTherapy describes monthly health reports and a one-time sharing code for a medication plan and intake diary.

Open a sample report before relying on either app. Check whether it shows actual intake times, skipped or missed entries, notes, symptoms, and the date range you want to discuss.

If interaction information matters

Medisafe currently lists a drug-to-drug interaction checker. MyTherapy's current public feature pages focus on reminders, refills, health journals, measurements, family profiles, and reports.

An app warning is not a substitute for a pharmacist or prescribing clinician. Interaction information can be incomplete, region-dependent, or different from advice for your specific health situation.

Where Medisafe fits better

  • You want a medication-first app with flexible or as-needed schedule support.
  • You want Medfriend alerts for a chosen family member or caregiver.
  • You want a publicly listed drug-interaction checker in the same app.
  • You want Apple Watch support listed in the App Store.

Where MyTherapy fits better

  • You want symptoms, pain, mood, and measurements beside medication records.
  • You want injection-site tracking.
  • You want reminders for measurements or appointments as well as medication.
  • You want a broader health journal rather than a mainly medication-first view.

When neither app may be enough

Neither choice should be based only on a long feature list. You may need a different tool if your priority is a pharmacy connection, a provider-managed care plan, a very specific caregiver workflow, or a specialist record that either app cannot export clearly.

If neither app fits the way you track ADHD medication, Pillr is built around reminders, dose history, focus windows, reflections, refill planning, and medication notes. It is a separate option, not part of the Medisafe and MyTherapy comparison above.

What to verify before choosing

  • Which reminder, medication, family, report, and refill features are free in your region.
  • Whether the current app supports every device you plan to use.
  • Whether you need an account for backup, sharing, family profiles, or moving to a new phone.
  • How the app handles health data, analytics, advertising, and deletion requests.
  • Whether the history and report show the exact details you need for appointments.
  • Whether your chosen care-partner setup requires consent, an invitation, or a paid plan.

Quick choice

  • Use Medisafe if care-partner alerts are the main need.
  • Use MyTherapy if medication and symptom tracking need to stay together.
  • Check paid limits before setting up family or refill features.

There is no universal winner. Choose the daily flow that matches the record you actually need.

Is Medisafe or MyTherapy better for medication reminders?

Both support medication reminders and dose records. Medisafe may fit better if care-partner alerts or a listed drug-interaction checker matter most. MyTherapy may fit better if you want symptom, pain, mood, and measurement tracking beside your medication routine.

Do Medisafe and MyTherapy support refill reminders?

Yes. Both apps currently describe medication supply tracking and refill or prescription reminders.

Can Medisafe and MyTherapy support family medication routines?

Both describe family features. Medisafe offers Medfriend and family support, while MyTherapy describes family profiles for shared care. Check the current paid options and setup rules in your region before choosing.

Do Medisafe or MyTherapy replace medical advice?

No. They are tracking and reminder tools. Questions about doses, timing, side effects, interactions, and missed medication need advice from a qualified healthcare professional.
