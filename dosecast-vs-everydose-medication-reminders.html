Dosecast vs EveryDose
for medication reminders

Dosecast and EveryDose both cover medication reminders, dose records, and flexible schedules. Dosecast puts more emphasis on interval timing and reminder control, while EveryDose includes medication information, interaction alerts, progress sharing, and a clearer split between standard and Plus features.

Published July 17, 2026 · Last reviewed July 20, 2026

A note from Pillr

This article is published by Pillr. We make a medication tracking app, but this page compares Dosecast and EveryDose for people deciding between those two options.

Quick answer

Choose Dosecast if you need unusually flexible reminder timing, including schedules based on the time since the last dose, and want to take, skip, or postpone a reminder without rebuilding the schedule.

Choose EveryDose if you want reminders and dose history beside medication leaflets, interaction alerts, progress sharing, and an optional paid health journal or family-profile setup.

Both are available on iPhone and Android, both can be downloaded free, and both reserve some refill, family, history, or reporting features for paid plans. Check the current store terms in your region before deciding.

Who this comparison is for

This comparison is for someone who wants a medication app to do more than ring at a fixed time. It is especially useful if your decision comes down to flexible intervals and reminder control versus built-in medication information and progress sharing.

It is not a guide to choosing medication, setting a dose, changing timing, or deciding what to do after a missed dose.

Key differences

Dosecast is more schedule-control led. Its current App Store page and Google Play page emphasise flexible intervals, reminders based on the time since the last dose, early actions, postponing, and time-zone handling.

EveryDose is more medication-information led. Its official feature page combines reminders and daily history with medication leaflets, interaction alerts, progress sharing, and optional Plus tools for health measurements, refills, and multiple profiles.

There is meaningful overlap. Both support reminders, flexible schedules, daily dose actions, medication lists, and paid upgrades. The more useful question is which tools need to be available before you pay.

Detailed comparison by use case

If your schedule is more complex than fixed times

Dosecast is the clearer fit when a reminder needs to repeat a set number of hours or days after the last recorded dose. It also describes daily, weekly, monthly, and every-few-days schedules, plus the ability to act early or postpone a reminder.

EveryDose supports daily, as-needed, specific-day, weekly, monthly, and cycle schedules. That covers many routines, but Dosecast gives interval timing a more central role in its current public information.

If dose history needs to be useful without an upgrade

EveryDose lists taken and skipped history in its standard feature set. Its current store information also describes medication-history export to a spreadsheet and a detailed medication-list PDF.

Dosecast lists detailed history, late or missed records, editing, and email sharing in its Pro edition. Its free edition still supports the immediate take, skip, and postpone actions, but the longer review workflow is part of the paid plan.

If refills or family tracking matter

Both apps place pill-count and refill reminders in their paid plans. Dosecast Pro also lists medication assignment for different people or pets. EveryDose Plus lists up to five additional profiles, while its standard progress-sharing feature can email reports to invited friends.

Choose based on the exact workflow. A separate profile for each person is different from sending a progress summary, and neither should be assumed to provide real-time caregiver oversight without checking the current setup.

If medication information matters

EveryDose lists medication leaflets, drug-to-drug interaction alerts, and food information in its standard features. Dosecast lists a drug database in Pro, mainly to make medication entry easier.

Neither app should be treated as the final word on interactions, side effects, missed doses, or timing. Confirm medication questions with a pharmacist or prescribing clinician.

If account and privacy setup matter

Dosecast says its free version does not require registration. Its Pro CloudSync feature moves medication data across devices, so review the current privacy policy before enabling it.

EveryDose's setup guide says an account can be skipped, while account creation enables sign-in and related services. Its store disclosures list data used for app functions, personalisation, analytics, and support. Read the current disclosures for the exact features you plan to use.

Where Dosecast fits better

  • You need reminder intervals based on the last recorded dose.
  • You want early, skip, and postpone actions to be central to the reminder flow.
  • You want basic use without registration.
  • You need Kindle availability or broad cross-platform CloudSync in a paid plan.

Where EveryDose fits better

  • You want taken and skipped history in the standard feature set.
  • You want medication leaflets and interaction alerts in the same app.
  • You want scheduled progress emails for a trusted person.
  • You want an optional paid health journal with measurements and charts.

When neither app may be enough

You may need another option if you want a local-only medication record, a pharmacy-managed workflow, a specific clinician portal, or an ADHD-focused routine with focus-window and reflection notes.

If neither app fits the way you track ADHD medication, Pillr is built around reminders, dose history, focus windows, reflections, refill planning, and medication notes. It is a separate option, not part of the Dosecast and EveryDose comparison above.

What to verify before choosing

  • Whether your exact schedule can be created without a workaround.
  • Which history, refill, family, and export tools are included in the current free plan.
  • Whether the paid plan works across every device you plan to use.
  • Whether registration, backup, or cloud sync is required for your chosen setup.
  • How the app handles medication data, analytics, support requests, and deletion.
  • Whether the report shows actual intake times, skipped entries, and the date range you need.

Quick choice

  • Use Dosecast if interval timing and reminder control are the main need.
  • Use EveryDose if medication information and progress sharing matter more.
  • Check paid limits before relying on refills, profiles, or detailed history.

There is no universal winner. Choose the daily flow that matches the record you actually need.

Is Dosecast or EveryDose better for flexible medication schedules?

Dosecast may fit better if interval schedules, early doses, or postponing reminders are central to your routine. EveryDose also supports daily, as-needed, specific-day, weekly, monthly, and cycle schedules.

Do Dosecast and EveryDose track medication history?

Yes, but the access differs. Dosecast lists detailed history and email sharing in its paid Pro edition. EveryDose lists taken and skipped history in its standard feature set, with spreadsheet export also available.

Do Dosecast and EveryDose support refill reminders?

Both list pill-count and refill tools as paid features. Check the current plan and store terms before relying on either app for supply tracking.

Do Dosecast or EveryDose replace medical advice?

No. They are tracking and reminder tools. Questions about doses, timing, side effects, interactions, and missed medication need advice from a qualified healthcare professional.
