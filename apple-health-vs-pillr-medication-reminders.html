Apple Health vs Pillr
for medication reminders

Apple Health Medications is built into Apple's Health app and can be enough for simple medication lists, schedules, reminders, and logs. Pillr is built for ADHD medication routines where reminders, dose history, focus windows, refill planning, and appointment notes need to stay together.

Last reviewed July 1, 2026

A note from Pillr

We make Pillr, so this is not an independent review. This page explains how we think about Apple Health Medications beside Pillr for ADHD medication routines.

Quick answer

Choose Apple Health Medications if you want a free, built-in Health app feature for a medication list, simple schedules, reminder notifications, and basic taken or skipped logging.

Choose Pillr if your main problem is ADHD medication follow-through: remembering the dose, logging what happened quickly, checking dose history later, tracking focus windows, planning refills, and bringing clearer notes to appointments.

Who this comparison is for

This page is for people comparing a built-in Apple medication feature with a dedicated ADHD medication tracker. It is especially relevant if you forget whether you took a dose, need a clearer dose history, want refill records, or want notes for medication review appointments.

It is not for Android medication reminders, caregiver medication management, pharmacy integrations, or deciding what medication to take.

How we think about the choice

Apple Health and Pillr are useful for different jobs. Apple Health is a built-in place to set up medication schedules, receive reminders, and log doses. Pillr is a dedicated ADHD medication tracker, so we focus on the parts of the routine that often need more structure: acting on reminders, logging quickly, checking history, planning refills, and preparing appointment notes.

We do not use public scores or overall rankings here. The better choice depends on what you need the app to help with.

Detailed comparison by use case

If you want simple built-in medication tracking

Apple Health Medications is a sensible first stop. Apple describes medication setup, schedules, reminders, taken or skipped logging, unscheduled medication logging, medication history review, and PDF export for a medication list.

If you mainly want a basic medication list inside the Health app, Apple Health may be enough.

If you want ADHD medication follow-through

Pillr is a stronger fit when the reminder is only one part of the problem. It combines medication reminders, quick dose logging, taken or skipped history, focus windows, refill planning, notes, and history exports in one focused app.

That matters when the question is not just "What time is my reminder?" but also "Did I take it?", "What happened this week?", "Am I running low?", and "What should I bring to my next appointment?"

If you want focus-window records

Pillr's Focus Timeline is built for stimulant medication routines. It uses your own onset and duration settings to show an estimated focus window beside your dose log. It should be treated as a planning and record-keeping feature, not as a medical prediction.

If you want appointment-ready medication history

Pillr is designed to help you review medication history, dose logs, notes, and exports before a clinician appointment. Apple Health may still be useful for a basic list, but Apple's medication guide describes PDF export for the medication list rather than a full ADHD routine history workflow.

Why Pillr is a strong fit

Pillr is strongest for adults with ADHD who want one focused app for reminders, dose logs, focus windows, refill planning, and appointment notes. It is also local-first: medication data is stored on the device, optional iCloud Drive backup is available, and no Pillr account is required.

Pillr does not tell you what medication to take, what dose to take, or how to respond to missed doses or side effects. It helps you keep a clearer record so you can discuss the right questions with a qualified healthcare professional.

When another option may be better

Apple Health Medications may be better if you want the simplest built-in Apple option, already use the Health app, or only need basic medication schedules and logs.

A broader medication app may be better if you need Android support, family or caregiver workflows, pharmacy or provider integrations, or chronic-condition tracking that is not mainly about ADHD medication routines.

What to verify before choosing

  • Whether the app is available in your country or region.
  • Whether the app supports your device and operating system.
  • Whether the export format gives you the history you need.
  • Whether reminders match your prescribed routine.
  • Whether privacy and backup settings match your comfort level.
  • Whether pricing or paid features have changed since this review.
