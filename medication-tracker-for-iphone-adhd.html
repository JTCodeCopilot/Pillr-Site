ADHD medication tracker

Speak with your doctor, psychiatrist, pharmacist, or prescribing clinician about medication timing, missed doses, side effects, or treatment decisions.

Start with the record you need

A useful ADHD medication tracker should answer three questions without making you reconstruct the day. It should also make repeat problems easier to notice.

  • What was due? The scheduled medication and reminder time.
  • What happened? Whether you logged the dose as taken or skipped.
  • What needs follow-up? A refill check, an unclear record, or a question for your clinician or pharmacist.

The National Institute of Mental Health describes ADHD as involving patterns such as difficulty staying organised, keeping on task, and getting things done. A clear external record can reduce the amount of routine information you have to hold in memory.

Use a small tracking rhythm

The tracker should stay useful across the whole routine, not only when a reminder appears.

Moment What to check What to record
When a reminder arrives The scheduled medication and time Taken or skipped, as soon as the action is complete
When you feel unsure later Today's dose history before relying on memory What the record shows and what remains unclear
During a weekly reset Supply, repeat interruptions, and unfinished notes A refill task or one question to follow up
Before an appointment The date range and medications you want to review A short summary or exported history

If a dose record is unclear, do not use the tracker to guess what medication action to take. Follow your own prescription instructions or ask a qualified clinician or pharmacist.

Keep medication history useful

A long history is not automatically a useful history. The best record is consistent and easy to scan. Keep the daily entry small:

  • Medication and scheduled time
  • Actual logged time
  • Taken or skipped status
  • A short note only when context matters
  • A symptom, side effect, or question you want to discuss

NICE ADHD guidance includes missed doses, adverse effects, and how treatment affects daily life among the areas considered during medication review. Tracking can help you bring a clearer record to that conversation, but it cannot interpret the record for you.

Why use a dedicated iPhone tracker?

Notes, calendar events, and generic reminders can each hold one part of a routine. A dedicated medication tracker keeps the reminder, the completed action, history, notes, and supply check together.

That matters when the problem is not only remembering a time. It is confirming what happened later or preparing a record for an appointment. If alerts are your main concern, use the ADHD medication reminder app checklist. If you are comparing built-in options, see Apple Health vs Pillr.

Where Pillr fits

Pillr is an ADHD medication reminder and dose tracking app for iPhone. It keeps medication reminders, taken or skipped dose logs, medication history, refill planning, and routine notes in one place.

Pillr can also export medication history as CSV or PDF. Before an appointment, you can choose the date range you need, review the record, and bring it into the conversation instead of relying on memory alone.

Prepare a clearer appointment record

Use this four-step check before a medication review:

  1. Choose the period you want to discuss.
  2. Review taken and skipped dose history for missing context.
  3. Add only the symptom, side-effect, refill, or routine notes that matter.
  4. Export the record and write down the questions that need a clinician's answer.

Pillr's My Appointment feature can also keep questions, symptoms or side effects, selected medications, and follow-up notes together. Those notes never change your medication, dose, schedule, or reminders automatically.

Know the privacy and safety boundaries

Pillr medication records stay on your device by default. Optional iCloud Drive backup is available, and no Pillr account is required.

Pillr does not tell you what medication to take, what dose to take, what to do after a missed dose, or when to change medication. Speak with your doctor, psychiatrist, pharmacist, or prescribing clinician about those decisions.

What should an ADHD medication tracker do?

It should make taken or skipped dose logging quick, keep a clear history, support short routine notes, help with refill checks, and make records easy to review.

What should I track before an ADHD medication appointment?

Useful records can include dose dates and times, taken or skipped status, short symptom or side-effect notes, refill questions, and anything you want to ask your clinician.

Can I export medication history from Pillr?

Yes. Pillr can export medication history as CSV or PDF so you can review it or share it during an appointment.

Can a tracker tell me what to do after a missed dose?

No. Follow your own prescription instructions and ask your doctor, psychiatrist, pharmacist, or prescribing clinician about missed-dose decisions.
