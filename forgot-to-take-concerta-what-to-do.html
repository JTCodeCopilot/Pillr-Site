Concerta guide

Forgot to take Concerta?
What to check next

If you forgot to take Concerta, the next decision depends on your own prescription instructions and advice from your clinician or pharmacist. Use this page to record what happened and what to ask, not to decide a dose action.

Updated June 28, 2026

This guide is for general information only and is not medical advice. Pillr helps with medication reminders, dose tracking, and routine notes, but it does not diagnose, treat, prevent, or cure ADHD. Speak with your doctor, psychiatrist, pharmacist, or prescribing clinician about medication timing, missed doses, side effects, or treatment decisions.

Record the timing before asking for advice

Write down your usual dose time, when you noticed the missed or uncertain dose, and what your prescription instructions say. If those instructions are unclear, ask a pharmacist or prescribing clinician before deciding what to do.

Why missed doses happen

Routine changes, rushed mornings, weekends, travel, and simple uncertainty are common reasons people miss a dose.

Missed-dose note template

Keep the note simple so it is useful later:

  • Medication: Concerta
  • Usual time and time noticed
  • What happened: taken, skipped, late, or unsure
  • Relevant context: sleep, food, travel, work, refill issue, side effects, or illness
  • Question for your pharmacist or prescribing clinician

How to make it less likely next time

  • Use a reminder that fits your real routine
  • Log the dose the moment it happens
  • Keep a clear history you can check later
  • Stay ahead of refills

Pillr is designed to help with that full routine, not just the reminder.

Where Pillr fits

Pillr is an ADHD medication reminder and dose tracking app for iPhone. It can help you set reminders, log taken or skipped doses, track supply, and review medication history.

Pillr does not tell you whether to take, skip, delay, or change a medication dose. Use your own prescription instructions and ask a pharmacist or prescribing clinician when unsure.
