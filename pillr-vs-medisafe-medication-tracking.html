Pillr vs Medisafe
for medication tracking

Pillr and Medisafe both combine medication reminders with dose records and refill tools. Pillr is focused on ADHD medication routines, while Medisafe is a broader medication manager with Android, Apple Watch, and care-partner support.

Published July 20, 2026 · Last reviewed July 23, 2026

A note from Pillr

We make Pillr, so this is not an independent review. This page explains how we compare Medisafe with Pillr for ADHD medication tracking and broader medication routines.

Quick answer

Choose Pillr if ADHD medication is the main routine you want to track and you want reminders, quick dose history, estimated focus windows, reflections, refill planning, and appointment notes in one local-first app.

Choose Medisafe if you want a general medication manager with Android and Apple Watch support, complex or as-needed schedules, Medfriend caregiver alerts, health measurements, reports, and a listed interaction checker.

Both can be downloaded free and both have paid options. The useful choice is not a universal winner; it is whether you need an ADHD-focused personal record or a broader medication and caregiver setup.

Who this comparison is for

This comparison is for someone deciding between an ADHD-focused medication tracker and a broader medication-management app. It is especially useful if your choice depends on focus-window records, reflections, caregiver alerts, Android support, refill tools, appointment records, or privacy setup.

It is not a guide to choosing medication, changing a dose, setting medical timing, or deciding what to do after a missed dose.

Key differences

Pillr is ADHD-routine led. It is built around quick taken or skipped records, focus windows based on personal settings, short focus, mood, symptom, and side-effect reflections, pill counts, and appointment preparation.

Medisafe is general medication-management led. Its current Google Play page lists complex and as-needed schedules, Medfriend, refill alerts, doctor appointments, reports, health measurements, and interaction information.

The overlap is real. Both support medication reminders, dose records, refills, reports, and interaction checks. The practical difference is the daily flow and who else needs access.

Detailed comparison by use case

If ADHD medication follow-through is the main job

Pillr is the clearer fit when you want a record that stays close to the ADHD medication routine. The reminder, dose log, estimated focus window, short reflection, refill status, and appointment notes can be reviewed together.

This is useful when the hard part is not only hearing an alert. It is also checking whether you logged the dose, seeing what you noticed later, and bringing a clearer history to a medication review.

If another person needs medication visibility

Medisafe is the stronger fit when a family member or caregiver needs a dedicated shared workflow. Its App Store page describes family medication sharing, while its privacy policy explains how invited Medfriends or caregivers may receive alerts and, when permitted, edit information.

Pillr has no caregiver account system. It is designed first as a personal local record, with user-initiated exports when someone wants to share medication history.

If schedules and device choice matter

Both support scheduled and as-needed medication. Medisafe gives broader public emphasis to complex schedules and is available on Android as well as Apple devices. It also lists Apple Watch support.

Pillr is Apple-focused and puts more attention on ADHD dose follow-through than on cross-platform family management. Check the current app-store compatibility before choosing, especially if several people or devices need the same setup.

If you want records for an appointment

Pillr combines medication history exports with My Appointment preparation, local appointment recording and transcription, an editable summary, and follow-up notes. Appointment audio, transcripts, and notes stay on the device.

Medisafe describes medication progress reports, PDF sharing, and a doctor appointment manager. That may be the better fit if you want broader medication reporting rather than a dedicated personal appointment record.

If account and privacy setup matter

Pillr has no Pillr account. Medication records stay on the device by default, with optional iCloud Drive backup. The Pillr privacy page explains optional features and analytics separately.

Medisafe says registration is not required for basic use. Its current privacy policy describes how personal and health information can be processed for reminders, sharing, connected services, subscriptions, and optional programs. Review the parts that match the features you plan to use.

Where Pillr fits better

  • ADHD medication is the main routine you want to track.
  • You want focus-window estimates and short post-dose reflections beside dose history.
  • You want a local-first personal medication record with no Pillr account.
  • You want medication history, refill planning, and appointment notes in one focused flow.

Where Medisafe fits better

  • You need Android or Apple Watch support.
  • You want Medfriend alerts or a broader family and caregiver workflow.
  • You manage several kinds of medication and want a general health measurement journal.
  • You want a long-established medication manager with broad schedule and report tools.

Why Pillr is a strong fit for ADHD medication tracking

Pillr is strongest when ADHD medication follow-through is the problem to solve. It keeps the reminder, the completed action, focus-window estimate, reflection, refill status, and appointment record close together instead of treating them as separate jobs.

It does not tell you what medication to take, what dose to take, or how to respond to missed doses or side effects. It helps you organise a clearer record for your own review and for conversations with a qualified healthcare professional.

What to verify before choosing

  • Whether the app supports every phone, tablet, or watch you plan to use.
  • Which medication, reminder, refill, report, and family features need a paid plan.
  • Whether another person needs alerts, editing access, or only an occasional export.
  • Whether the history shows the actual times, skipped entries, and notes you need.
  • How backup, analytics, sharing, and deletion work for your chosen setup.
  • Whether interaction information is available in your region and covers your medication list.

Is Pillr or Medisafe better for ADHD medication tracking?

Pillr may fit better if you want ADHD-focused dose records, focus windows, reflections, refill planning, and appointment notes. Medisafe may fit better if you need Android support, Apple Watch support, Medfriend caregiver alerts, or broader medication-management tools.

Do Pillr and Medisafe require an account?

Pillr has no Pillr account and keeps medication records on the device by default. Medisafe says basic use does not require registration, while backup, sharing, and other connected features may have their own setup.

Do Pillr and Medisafe support refill tracking?

Yes. Both currently describe pill supply or refill tracking. Paid-feature boundaries differ, so check the current app-store terms before relying on either setup.

Do Pillr or Medisafe replace medical advice?

No. They are tracking and reminder tools. Questions about doses, timing, side effects, interactions, and missed medication need advice from a qualified healthcare professional.
